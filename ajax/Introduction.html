Introduction

Trees are fun. Often trees can disguise their wonder. Even basic research can show how these plants are at least as fascinating as the extroverted animal kingdom. This key was made to help kindle interest in trees and be the beginnings of an education into their intriguing world.

This key is by no means an exhaustive list of the trees of the British Isles, but more of an overview of the more common trees likely to be seen in the wild. The key is designed for use by people with some botanical knowledge, but it is still navigable by those without using the glossary (and patience). Trees grown in cultivation can be identified, however, their morphology may be different so identification may be harder. With these aspects in mind, plenty of fun can be had out in the field identifying trees to your hearts content and realising your botanical potential!

This key was made using Bentham and Hooker’s Handbook of British Flora and the accompanying book of illustrations. Bentham first published this key in 1858 with the aim to enable people with no prior botanical knowledge to “name the wild flowers they may gather on their country rambles” (Bentham and Hooker, 1943), and was Bentham’s most famous work. It forms the basis for this key as it is a classic key that has been used by students for over a century running into many editions, it is now out of print.
